Good Shopify design is **constraint-aware**: every promo rail, countdown, and editorial block has to survive **real catalog variance**, **translations**, and **theme updates**. We design with those constraints visible from week one—not as surprises during build.

We collaborate in **Figma** (or your tool of choice) with **named components**, **responsive frames**, and **annotation for metafields and dynamic sources**. That reduces the classic gap where a beautiful desktop comp cannot be expressed as maintainable sections.

We also align UX with **measurement**: which modules support experimentation, where trust signals belong on PDP, and how navigation scales when you add markets or B2B catalogs.

### What it does

- **UX and UI design** for core commerce journeys on Shopify.
- **Design QA** against real product data samples—not only idealized SKUs.
- **Documentation** for developers: spacing, interaction notes, and edge states (empty cart, sold out, backorder).
- **Optional art direction** coordination for photography and motion briefs.

### Who it's for

- Marketing-led brands that need **design and engineering to speak one language**.
- Merchants whose current theme **looks dated** but who cannot afford a fragile bespoke rebuild.
- Teams preparing a **rebrand** timed with a platform or catalog overhaul.
