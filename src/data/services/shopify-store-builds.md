We treat a Shopify launch as **revenue infrastructure**, not a brochure. That means decisions about **collections, URL strategy, metafields, markets, apps, and checkout** are made with your merchandising and ops reality in mind—not bolted on after go-live.

Discovery covers catalog shape, content sources, ERP or 3PL constraints, and what “success” means on day thirty—not only day one. We document assumptions, edge cases, and rollback thinking so launch week stays boring in the best way.

During build we favor **Online Store 2.0** patterns merchants can extend: section presets, metaobjects for editorial rails, and performance budgets on the templates paid traffic hits first. For migrations we reconcile **redirects, metafields, customer import policies, and subscription or B2B** scenarios early so you are not debugging them under fire.

### What it does

- **Greenfield storefronts** — IA, design handoff to Liquid, cart and PDP patterns, policy pages, and analytics baselines.
- **Replatforming** — phased or big-bang cutovers with SEO preservation, inventory truth, and post-launch monitoring.
- **Markets expansion** — domains, currencies, duties messaging, and catalog rules that match legal and finance input.
- **Launch readiness** — QA scripts for checkout, shipping profiles, and high-risk promos before you scale media spend.

### Who it's for

- Founders and ecommerce leads who need **one accountable build partner**.
- Operators who already know their catalog is **messy** and want it untangled before replatforming.
- Brands that expect **iteration after launch** and want architecture that will not fight them.
